Australia Abandons Plans for an AI Advisory Body, Moving to a More Flexible Approach

Category: Governance

Having invested over a year in recruiting experts for a planned AI Advisory Body announced in 2024, the federal government in Australia announced it will instead establish a new AI Safety Institute. The change signals a transition from potentially mandatory AI guardrails to a more flexible governance approach.

Observers suggest the shift may reflect broader global debates about regulatory approaches to AI. While the U.S. has promoted more “enabling” approaches, the European Union has advanced a tighter regulatory environment. Reporters believe Australia’s decision is part of a change in strategy toward a more flexible and potentially more lenient AI regulatory approach.

Implication: Affects international research collaboration.
