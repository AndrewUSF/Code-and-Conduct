February 2026 Edition

AI is no longer regulated at the margins. Legal and regulatory developments are beginning to shape core technical decisions, from model training and dataset construction to evaluation, deployment, and access controls.

Questions once confined to law reviews are now operational: Can training on publicly available data trigger IP liability? When does an AI system's output create tort, contract, or copyright risk? What documentation, risk assessment, or transparency obligations attach to high-impact systems?

New AI Literacy Framework by the Department of Labor

The Department of Labor has released its Artificial Intelligence Literacy Framework, encouraging education and workplace systems to integrate AI literacy in an impactful manner.

Implication: May influence curriculum design and federal funding alignment.

German Ruling Denied Copyright Protection for Logos Produced by GenAI

A local court in Munich dismissed a copyright claim asserting protection over logos generated by GenAI, emphasizing that copyright requires original human creation.

Implication: Relevant for commercialization and IP strategy.

NY Federal Judge Asserts AI-Generated Legal Documents Are Not Privileged

A federal judge in the Southern District of New York indicated that documents generated through AI tools and later shared with attorneys may not be protected by attorney-client privilege.

Implication: Caution advised when using AI for legal drafting.

Read More →

Australia Establishes AI Safety Institute

Australia will replace its planned AI Advisory Body with an AI Safety Institute, signaling a transition from mandatory AI guardrails to a more flexible governance approach.

Implication: Affects international research collaboration.
