New AI Literacy Framework by the Department of Labor

The Department of Labor recently released its Artificial Intelligence Literacy Framework, encouraging education systems and workplaces to integrate AI literacy in a practical and measurable way.

The framework emphasizes five foundational skills, including understanding AI principles and using AI responsibly, alongside seven delivery strategies such as experiential learning, learning in context, and design for agility.

Implication: May influence curriculum design and federal funding alignment.

German Ruling Denied Copyright Protection for Logos Produced by GenAI

The local Court in Munich dismissed a copyright claim earlier this February, that purported to assert copyright protection over three logos generated by GenAI. The court, in one of the first European rulings on the matter, held that the logos were not eligible for copyright protection, even though a human allegedly invested many hours in refining the underlying prompts. According to the ruling, copyright protection could theoretically have been granted where despite the use of AI, the design of the logo was the result of a “human creative influence”, and the AI was used as a tool rather than an “independent creative instrument”.

Implication: GenAI output might potentially be copyrightable under very specific circumstances.

NY Federal Judge Asserts AI-Prepared Legal Documents Are Not Subject to Attorney-Client Privilege

Attorney-client privilege protects confidential communications between clients and their lawyers. In February, a federal judge in the Southern District of New York stated during a hearing that documents generated through an AI tool for legal purposes were not privileged.

The court accepted the government's position, whose arguments were based on the fact that the AI tool was not an attorney and that either way its terms of use disclaimed legal advice and privilege.

Implication: Caution advised when using AI for legal drafting.
